In early 20th century Canada, the first portable automatic saws eliminated the need for manpower to cut logs. A simple 5 horsepower hit-and- miss engine powered a long saw blade that sliced through logs with surprising efficiency, revolutionizing carpentry by cutting in minutes what once took hours without ever having to lift the saw.
