Video Title: Why Cork Was Used in Classic Old Shoe Soles
View Count: 27,408,334

Why were the soles of old classic shoes filled with cork? And why all the effort? Back in the 1700s, cork became popular in Europe for its lightness, flexibility, and natural shockabsorbing qualities. It quickly became a go-to material for shoe soles. Even better, it was a sustainable resource harvested from tree bark without ever cutting the tree
