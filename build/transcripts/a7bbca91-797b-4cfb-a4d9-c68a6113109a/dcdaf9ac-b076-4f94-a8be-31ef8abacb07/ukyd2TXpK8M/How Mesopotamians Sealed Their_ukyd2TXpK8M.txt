Video Title: How Mesopotamians Sealed Their Identity
View Count: 317,446

This tiny object used in ancient Mesopotamia had an incredible purpose. These small cylinders, usually made of precious stone, were carved with detailed imagery or inscriptions. Used like a signature or stamp, the seal was rolled over soft clay like that found on tablets or bags, transferring the carved image or inscription. In doing so, it marked ownership and protected the good's authenticity.
